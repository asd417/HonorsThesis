
\section{Automatic Generation of Sokoban Puzzles}
One of the earliest academic research on Sokoban afterwards was on generating Sokoban puzzles. The researchers framed the work as an attempt to generate art with computers, arguing that creation of art by computer is an important target of artificial intelligence. \cite{murase1996automatic} Little did the researchers realize that in the current years of 2025, art generated by computer is being met with heavy criticism \cite{AIArt} and automatic generation of game content is often framed more as a method to create data for reinforcement learning.\cite{bento2019procedural} Regardless of their intention, Murase et al formed the foundation for generating dataset for Sokoban agent. \cite{murase1996automatic}

The method with which the researchers achieved automatic generation of Sokoban level is through a 3-step methodology. First, candidates for problems are generated randomly by a prototype and three templates. Next, unsolvable candidates are removed by a Sokoban solver. Finally trivial or uninteresting candidates are removed by the evaluator. Templates are 2x3, 2x2, and 3x3 empty spaces to encourage generation of multiple paths inside the space. After placing tiles, (Figure \ref{fig:ag1} ) there are some tiles where the box can never be placed. These tiles are marked as GOAL\_AVOID (Figure \ref{fig:autogeneration2} ). Goal candidate tiles that are in corners are marked as COR\_GOAL and the next goal is placed on goal candidate tiles that are not deadends or COR\_GOAL. Deadend areas ADD\_AVOID areas are recalculated after every placement.

After the generation of the level, a Breadth-First-Search is used to solve the puzzle. While the BFS solver is not able to find solution to the puzzle with long sequences, it can still effectively search for the solution with short sequences. \cite{fryers1995sokoban} Puzzles that can not be solved are pruned in this step. 

The most crucial contribution of this paper is the metric to determine whether the generated puzzle is non-trivial and interesting. The evaluation program aims evaluate artistic values using following criteria:

\begin{center}
    \begin{minipage}{0.8\textwidth} % Adjust width as needed
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Length of solution sequence: if a problem has a very short solution sequence, it is trivial and is rejected. when the length of a solution sequence of a problem is less than seven, the problem is rejected.
            \item The number of changes in directions of pushing in solution sequence: in interesting problems the keeper has to change the directions of pushing objects very often. if the number of the changes are less than four, it is trivial and must be removed.
            \item The number of detours in solution sequence: if a solution sequence of a problem has no detours, it is uninteresting and is rejected.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{minipage}
\end{center}

The authors claim that these criteria guides generation of interesting puzzles.
